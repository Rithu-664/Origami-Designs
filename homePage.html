Camel

Interesting facts about Camels!

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. Camels can reach 7 feet in height (at the hump) and weigh up to 1500 pounds.
  3. Camel's foot consists of two toes that spread when animal touches the ground and prevents sinking in the sand. Camels are mostly famous for their humps.

Flying Cicada

Interesting facts about Flying cicadas!

  1. Most flying cicadas have red-orange eyes.
  2. Cicadas are great at clinging to tree trunks and making loud screeching sounds by vibrating their bodies. But these bulky, red-eyed insects aren't so great at flying.
  3. Cicadas are herbivores. This means they eat vegetation. Young cicadas eat liquid from plant roots, while molting cicadas eat twigs.

Chameleon

Interesting facts about Chameleons!

  1. Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  2. Most chameleons eat on things you wouldn't want to find on your dinner plate.
  3. Chameleons also can see ultraviolet light, which isn’t visible to humans.

Panda

Interesting facts about Pandas!

  1. They have great camouflage for their environment.
  2. Bamboo is critical to their diet.
  3. All Giant Pandas have black patches around their eyes and black ears on a white head. Their legs are black and there is a black band across their backs.

Pigeon

Interesting facts about Pigeons!

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  2. Pigeons are highly sociable animals.
  3. In captivity, pigeons commonly live up to 15 years and sometimes longer.

Dog

Interesting facts about Dogs!

  1. Their sense of smell is at least 40x better than ours.
  2. Dogs can sniff at the same time as breathing
  3. Some are fast and could even beat a cheetah!

Baby Penguin

Interesting facts about Baby Penguins!

  1. A group of penguins in the water is called a raft but on land they're called a waddle!
  2. Penguins evolved to fly underwater.
  3. The black and white “tuxedo” look donned by most penguin species is a clever camouflage called countershading.

Owl

Interesting facts about Owls!

  1. Many owl species have asymmetrical ears.
  2. Owls can rotate their necks 270 degrees.
  3. A group of owls is called a parliament.

Koala

Interesting facts about Koala!

  1. Koalas are not bears—they're marsupials.
  2. Koalas can sleep up to 18 hours a day.
  3. An adult koala eats about 1/2 - 1 kilogram of leaves each night.

Brachiosaurus

Interesting facts about Brachiosaurus!

  1. The Brachiosaurus is a type of dinosaur known as a Sauropod, they are famous for reaching incredible sizes.
  2. Brachiosaurus had 26 teeth on its top jaw and 26 on the bottom for a total of 52 teeth towards the front of the mouth.
  3. Brachiosaurus were responsible for a lifespan of nearly 100 years.

Hippopotamus

Interesting facts about Hippopotamus!

  1. They are considered the second largest land animal on Earth.
  2. Males measure around 3.5m long and 1.5m tall, and can weigh up to 3,200kg.
  3. Hippos are most active at night, when they forage for food.

Fox

Interesting facts about Fox!

  1. A group of foxes is called a skulk or leash.
  2. Foxes have whiskers on their legs and face, which help them to navigate.
  3. A male is called a 'dog fox' while a female is called a 'vixen'

Kitty

Interesting facts about Kitty!

  1. Cats can rotate their ears 180 degrees.
  2. The hearing of the average cat is at least five times keener than that of a human adult.
  3. In the largest cat breed, the average male weighs approximately 20 pounds.

Crab

Interesting facts about Crab!

  1. There are over 6,700 species of crab.
  2. The Japanese spider crab is the world's largest crab, measuring 12 to 13 feet across.
  3. A group of crabs is called a cast.

Frog

Interesting facts about Frog!

  1. Frogs can lay as many as 4,000 eggs in frogspawn.
  2. Frogs have long back legs and webbed feet for jumping and swimming.
  3. Most frogs have teeth, although usually only on their upper jaw.
